What is hit location?

In a role playing game, otherwise known as table top RPG, players take on different characters and ‘act’ out roles according to set rules. Depending on the number of players the game allows (it could be a single player or a multiplayer game), these players go on quests, come across monsters on their way, and make use of hit location dices to take them down by hitting them at target points.

Usually, in games like this, there is a Game Master (GM) that sets out the rules of the game. As a player, you attack by specifying the target point on your opposition’s body. It could be his head, torso, arms and legs. At the point where the attacker aims, he would need to add the aiming penalty to his hit role. For each target hit, the attacker rolls only the particular area for a hit, randomly.

Hit locations may either be general or specific.

Typical approaches and issues.

Many games simply ignore and only use hit-points to measure damage, this approach is not too realistic.

Players could use simple hit location dice. It is a quick way and gives a simple result. It can be rigid as not include the circumstances.

Some players use hit location tables which can easily can get complicated and need multiple dice roll which could slow down gameplay. Or if too simple, then rigid and inaccurate.
